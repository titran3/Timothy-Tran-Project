Low-Income School Areas

Compared to schools in higher-income neighborhoods, low-income schools have less opportunities. High levels of science, math, and advanced placement courses are less accessible to students in high poverty schools, and state and spending on teachers and educational resources is lower. In the 2013–2014 academic year, the average teacher compensation in high poverty schools was around $46,000 as opposed to approximately $57,000 in low poverty schools.

Children from low-income families often do not receive the stimulation and do not learn the social skills required to prepare them for school.

MLK is one of the lowest-income schools in the Bay Area. Most students are low income students and a ⅓ are English learners.

The greatest COVID impact was on low-income schools. The pandemic altered the graduation plans of 30% of QC students, with two fifths of them delaying graduation, and decreased freshmen student retention by 26%. Additionally, the pandemic cost 39% of the students their jobs, cut their salaries by 35%, and decreased their predicted household income by 64%.
